Wheel Pose

Category: Backbend Level: Advanced Sanskrit: Urdhva Dhanurasana

Step-by-Step Guide

1
Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat near hips
2
Place hands beside ears, fingers pointing toward shoulders
3
Inhale and press up, lifting hips toward ceiling
4
Straighten arms and legs as much as possible
5
Let head hang freely between arms
6
Hold 5–10 breaths, lower down slowly

Benefits

Intense backbend that opens the entire front body. Strengthens arms, legs and spine. Energizes and elevates mood.

Pro Tips

Warm up thoroughly with Bridge Pose and Camel Pose before attempting Wheel. Keep elbows shoulder-width and don't let them splay.

FAQ

Q: I can't press up into Wheel Pose — what should I practice first?

Master Bridge Pose, Camel Pose and Locust Pose first. Then try pressing halfway up and holding (Baby Wheel) to build the strength needed.
